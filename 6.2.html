STEP ONE

Erect signage and barricades

Erect signage and barricades.

Some wall frames will need to be craned into place. Ensure you have safety signs and barricades in place prior to delivery.


STEP TWO

Inspect wall frames

Inspect wall frames.

When the wall frames arrive on site, report any missing components, damaged parts or manufacturing mistakes to your supervisor immediately in case replacements or repairs need to be made by the manufacturer.


STEP THREE

Stack wall frames

Stack the wall frames in accordance with the manufacturers' recommendations and instructions.

Stack the wall frames so that the one you’ll be erecting first is at the top of the stack and the one you’ll use last is at the bottom of the stack. You may find it easy to stack the internal wall frames on the slab and the external wall frames on the perimeter of the structure.

The manufacturer will usually supply instructions and recommendations for their safe storage on site, e.g. blocking to prevent contact with the ground.

Tip: Most manufacturer’s use a numbering system which means they will number the panels and junctions during the fabrication process.

Be familiar with the numbering system and always check the numbers stamped on the panels and junctions match the numbers on the plans.


STEP FOUR

Check measurements

Check the measurements of the slab or sub-floor.

Before setting out, check the measurements of the slab or sub-floor in case mistakes have been made during construction. You may need to make adjustments when setting out the wall frames if the slab or sub-floor is too big, too small or uneven.

Don't make changes that impact the structural integrity of the building.

The manufacturer will usually supply instructions and recommendations for their safe storage on site, e.g. blocking to prevent contact with the ground.

Tip: Most manufacturer’s use a numbering system which means they will number the panels and junctions during the fabrication process.

Be familiar with the numbering system and always check the numbers stamped on the panels and junctions match the numbers on the plans.


STEP FIVE

Set out location of walls

Set out the location of the walls on the slab or sub-floor.

Refer to the plans, drawings and specifications for measurements.

Tip: One way to set out the location of the walls is to start by marking the longest wall in the plans with a straight line. Mark a second line for the wall frame that joins at right angles to the first and check these two lines for square. Mark the remaining wall frames with lines that are parallel to the first or second lines.

The manufacturer will usually supply instructions and recommendations for their safe storage on site, e.g. blocking to prevent contact with the ground.

Tip: Most manufacturer’s use a numbering system which means they will number the panels and junctions during the fabrication process.

Be familiar with the numbering system and always check the numbers stamped on the panels and junctions match the numbers on the plans.


STEP SIX

Follow special requirements

Follow the manufacturers' special requirements (if required).

Take note of any special requirements in manufacturers’ recommendations and instructions.

For example, if you’re erecting wall frames on a slab, you may need to lay a damp proof course e.g. polyethylene, to prevent contact with the slab.
